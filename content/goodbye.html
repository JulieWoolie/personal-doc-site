For they who doth care,

I write this letter in the same manner which I lived: Mediocre and behind the times. In a world in which I had not failed miserably to apply myself, to become who I dreamed of being, this letter would not have been written. Instead, I would have been with friends, or a partner, or partners. Who knows what an iteration of me who is not burdened with despair would look like.

I dreamed of writing epics, of creating art or programs, of speaking a dozen languages, or being at the head of some metaphorical or literal ship. However, that is all I appear to be: A dreamer. I dreamed of marble halls and golden chairs, clothes of exquisite weave and make. Yet I lack the will, or the strength or the drive to see that my life be one of the grandeur I dream of.

At the top of it all my failures, many of which I can only blame myself for, there lays my inability to connect, or to trust another human. I witnessed all those I called friends grow up. I saw them make friends, find partners in a manner I could not dream of, at a speed with which I could not match. I can pound the wall with my fist and scream at all the causes I perceive to have removed my ability to connect, but it matters not. I've delayed enough, and I've lost too much time. All I see myself able to do, is mourn, before it all ends.

Make no mistake, I enjoyed moments, moments when I was able to be happy around others, able to share in banter, although that was rare. But enjoyment of rare moments does not offset the failures of the majority of my time.

The last comfort I shall have, is that I will be forgotten. If I may be granted one thing, it's that I be forgotten, my name be erased and a void left where I was. That hope of being erased, gives me great comfort. It's shame, I think. I failed and for that I feel such great shame that it gives me comfort to know my failure might be forgotten. My sins burned away.

To those that knew me closely, forgive me. I know this action to be one of the most selfish I could perform, but, when, in reality, have I ever been anything but? Thank you for providing me comfort, joy, and some faint sense of connection and belonging before the end. Were it not for you, I may have departed far sooner.

The forest, the winter air and all the blasted snow will be cold and it'll bite at me. But at least, I will have fucking died somewhere other than the bedroom I rotted away in, the bedroom I wasted so much of my time in.


Best regards,
Julie
